PROTOTYPE -- SPOKE REDESIGN
Before / After: Scope vs. Spoke Grouping
The current model couples scope selection (which data to show) with spoke grouping (how to arrange it). The redesign decouples them into two independent controls.
Current: Pick one entity, spokes auto-assigned Current
Select dimension (TA/Company/MOA/ROA), then pick one entity. Spokes are the cross-dimension. Cannot mix categories.
Proposed: Filter scope freely, group by any axis Proposed
Multi-select filters define which assets appear. A separate toggle controls spoke grouping. Regroup instantly without re-fetching.
Current UX flow
4 steps to see the chart
1 Pick dimension tab (TA, Company...)
2 Browse entity index grid
3 Click one entity to load chart
4 Use filters to narrow within that entity
To compare Oncology + Immunology: navigate back, pick the other TA, open in a second tab
Proposed UX flow
2 steps, then explore
1 Filter to the data you care about (multi-select)
2 Pick spoke grouping (Company, Indication, MOA...)
Toggle grouping instantly. Add/remove filters without leaving the chart. Cross-dimension views are native.

Usage Scenarios
Each scenario shows a question a CI analyst might ask, how they would configure the chart, and what they see.
Scenario A: "Who is competing in Oncology + Immunology?"
Multi-indication competitive landscape
Scope: Indication = Oncology, Immunology
Group by: Company
Result: 7 spokes (companies active in either TA), 42 asset dots. Instant comparison of pipeline depth across companies spanning both areas.
Not possible today: would require opening two separate bullseye views and mentally merging them.
Scenario B: "How does Pfizer's oral portfolio compare across indications?"
Single company, filtered by ROA, grouped by indication
Scope: Company = Pfizer, ROA = Oral
Group by: Indication
Result: Spokes are indications where Pfizer has oral assets. Shows which therapeutic areas Pfizer is pushing oral formulations into.
Today: pick Company dimension, select Pfizer, then filter by ROA. But spokes are TAs, not indications (post-redesign these are separate). Close but not the same cross-cut.
Scenario C: "What does the PD-1/PD-L1 landscape look like?"
MOA-centric view, grouped by company
Scope: MOA = PD-1/PD-L1
Group by: Company (default), then toggle to Indication
Result: First view: which companies are developing checkpoint inhibitors and how far along. Toggle to Indication grouping: which diseases are being targeted with this mechanism.
Today: pick MOA dimension, select PD-1/PD-L1. Spokes are auto-assigned. No toggle to regroup.
Scenario D: "Show me everything in this space"
Full portfolio overview, no scope filters
Scope: (none -- all assets)
Group by: Company
Result: Full competitive landscape. If >20 spokes, auto-collapse to top 15 by asset count + "Other" spoke. User can then progressively filter to narrow.
Today: not possible. Must pick one entity first. The index grid is the closest, but it is not a chart.
Pitfall: Spoke explosion
What happens when there are too many spokes?
Problem: No filters + group by Company could yield 40+ spokes. Labels overlap, sectors narrow, dots collide.

Mitigations (pick one or combine):
A. Auto-cap at 15 spokes. Remaining collapse into "Other (25 more)". Click to expand.
B. Require at least one scope filter before rendering. Show an empty state: "Filter by indication, company, or mechanism to see the bullseye."
C. At >20 spokes, auto-suggest switching to matrix/heatmap view.
D. Progressive disclosure: start zoomed to a quadrant, scroll/rotate to see more.
Pitfall: Duplicate asset dots
An asset in 3 indications appears on 3 spokes when grouped by indication
Problem: Keytruda (Merck) is approved in 15+ indications. Grouped by indication, it appears 15 times.

Mitigations:
A. Accept duplication (analytically correct). Count badge says "42 assets (38 unique)".
B. Outline duplicate dots with a dashed ring to signal they appear elsewhere.
C. On hover, highlight all copies of the same asset across spokes.
D. "Unique placement" toggle: assign each asset to its highest-phase spoke only.
