Where we are

Surfacing Stout's primary intelligence layer in Clint.

We started with one question (where is Stout's primary intelligence on the timeline?), then walked into where else intelligence belongs in the product, what schema changes that needs, what role PPTs play, and what to ship first. This page collects all of it. Decisions on top, sketches in the middle, open questions and next moves at the bottom.

01

Decisions made

Locked, not stale
Accept
Marker treatment: variant B (halo)

Thin brand-700 ring around analyst-curated markers on the timeline. Preserves category color. Lightest move that telegraphs Stout's curation at a glance.

Accept
Halo trigger: presence of analyst description

Halo flips on when a marker has commentary attached. No new schema flag. Analysts upgrade a CT.gov-synced marker just by writing a description.

Accept
"Linked" replaces "comparators"

Section header is "Linked." Each chip carries a typed sub-label (Competitor, Predecessor, Combination, Same-class, etc.) so the relationship kind is explicit.

Accept
Authoring: structured shell, markdown bodies

Trial-detail and catalyst reads have typed fields (thesis, what to watch, linked entities, implications by lane, lifecycle status). Each prose body is markdown. Inline entity references render as chips. Marker descriptions are just markdown.

Accept
Latest from Stout = recently-published reads

No separate notice entity, no notification flag. Every published read appears in the Latest from Stout feed. If Stout publishes it, the client sees it. Pure thesis updates without a real-world event are markers too, just analyst-created. Email distribution is handled at the engagement subscription level, not per-publish.

Accept
Draft / Published is the only visibility state

Reads have two states. Draft is visible to the Stout team only; analysts can work-in-progress without exposing half-formed thinking to the client. Published is visible to everyone in the engagement and lands in Latest from Stout. Drafts are discoverable from a "Your drafts" card on the engagement landing (Stout team only).

Accept
PPTs / docs: Clint is the registry, not the author

PPTs and docs continue to be made in PowerPoint and Word, distributed via email and meetings the way they are today. Clint logs each artifact, attaches it to the entities it relates to, and becomes the institutional memory for the engagement. No PPT export, no live-link add-in.

Accept
Single registry, three types

One materials list per entity, recency-ordered, visible to everyone in the engagement. No visibility lanes. Three types: Briefing, Priority Notice, Ad Hoc. Truly internal client documents stay in their own corporate systems, not in Clint.

Accept
Schema additions on the marker

Implication (BD / Strategy / Licensing lanes), linked entities, lifecycle status (developing / monitoring / resolved / superseded), and revision history.

Accept
Markdown editor: ProseMirror, kept simple for v1

v1 ships with paragraphs, bold, italic, bulleted and numbered lists, and links. Markdown serialization via prosemirror-markdown. Deferred to v2: @-typed entity reference autocomplete, inline entity chip rendering, and material reference chips. Structured fields (Linked entities chip picker, Headline, Lifecycle status) stay outside the prose editor and ship in v1.

Accept
Implications: bullets, no lanes

One bulleted list of implications, like "What to watch." No BD / Strategy / Licensing lane structure. Drops the lane taxonomy from the schema and the open question on what those lanes should be.

Accept
Engagement landing composition

Two-column dashboard. Main column carries Latest from Stout (notices feed) and Recent materials. Side rail carries Stout drafts (agency-only) and Next 14 days catalysts. Top bar is whitelabel-branded with Stout attribution. Engagement context strip shows live stats.

Accept
Engagement landing is the default route on space entry

Replaces the timeline as the page users land on when clicking into a space. Same default for agency and client. Timeline moves to a tab (`/t/:tenant/s/:space/timeline`). Onboarding tooltip on first post-deploy login pointing users to the new Timeline tab.

Reject
curated_by_role field

No schema field tracking who authored a marker (sync vs. analyst vs. client). Description-driven trigger replaces this signal cleanly.

Reject
conviction / confidence field

No structured confidence rating. Conviction stays in the prose voice of the analyst. Terse means confident; hedged means watching.

Dissolve
Briefing as a separate Clint surface

The deliverable briefing PPT is still authored in PowerPoint. What lives in Clint is the continuously-updated published reads on entity surfaces. No standalone briefing artifact to build.

04

Open questions

Need a call before building
Taxonomy
Implication lanes: BD / Strategy / Licensing.

Used in the trial-detail sketch as the three structured lanes for analyst implications. Confirm the taxonomy before baking it into the schema. Possible additions: Regulatory, Commercial, Portfolio.

Materials
Preview depth and ingest method.

Slide-1 thumbnail vs. full slide carousel. Manual upload only vs. email-in / Google Drive / SharePoint connectors. Probably staged: upload + thumbnail first, connectors later.

Authoring
Markdown editor choice.

TipTap, ProseMirror, or PrimeNG Editor as the foundation for the structured + markdown authoring surface. Custom node types needed for inline entity references and material references.

Subscriptions
Engagement-level email distribution.

With per-publish notification gone, distribution becomes a subscription setting per engagement. Who's on the list, what cadence (every publish, daily digest, weekly digest), what channel (email is required, Slack is a maybe). Configure once at engagement setup, no decision at publish time.
