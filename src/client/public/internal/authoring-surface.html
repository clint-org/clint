Design notes. Side drawer at 720px wide. Underlying page stays visible behind the dim so analysts maintain spatial context. Save state lives in the header (auto-saving drafts every few seconds). Draft pill is amber to distinguish work-in-progress from a published read; once published, the pill turns brand-700.

Markdown editor with structure. Each prose field is a markdown editor (TipTap or ProseMirror in production), supporting bold, italic, lists, links, and custom inline nodes for entity references and material references. The "@ Entity" toolbar action and the @-typed autocomplete are the same primitive: a unified picker that ranges over companies, products, trials, markers, materials. Result is inserted as a chip that stays linked to the entity even if the entity is renamed.

Linked field is structured, not free text. The chip picker sits at the bottom of the drawer body. Each row has the entity, its relationship type to this read (Competitor, Same class, Predecessor, Combination, Future window, Partner, etc.), and a remove action. The relationship type is what makes the cross-entity navigation meaningful downstream.

Implications lanes can stay empty. Three lanes (BD, Strategy, Licensing). Any subset can be filled. The "Licensing" lane in this sketch is empty, with a "+ Add" affordance. This is the structured shell pattern: the slot exists so the data is queryable, but it's not coercing the analyst to fabricate content.

Draft and published states are the only visibility lever. A draft is visible to the Stout team only. Publishing flips the read to visible-to-everyone-in-the-engagement and adds it to the Latest from Stout feed. There's no separate notify-on-publish toggle; if Stout published it, the client sees it. Email distribution to recipients is handled at the engagement subscription level (out of scope for the drawer), not as a per-publish decision. Drafts are discoverable from the engagement landing's "Your drafts" card (Stout team only) so analysts can pick up where they left off.
