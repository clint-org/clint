Design notes. One flat list, recency-ordered. Anything in the registry is visible to everyone in the engagement; truly internal documents stay in the client's own corporate systems, not in Clint. Each row carries enough metadata to decide whether to open it: type, uploader, date, page or slide count, distribution stamp, linked entities. Slide-1 thumbnails are stylized placeholders here; in production they render server-side via LibreOffice headless or CloudConvert.

Three types. Briefing (the cadence-driven analytical deliverable, like the quarterly), Priority Notice (the interrupt-driven note Stout sends in response to an event or thesis change), Ad Hoc (everything else: diligence, transcripts, board reads, one-off memos, client-uploaded decks). Three is enough. We can split if a fourth pattern emerges in real use.

Client-uploaded materials are first-class. The Pfizer board read (row 4) is uploaded by D. Reyes at Pfizer, marked with a Pfizer-blue uploader name to distinguish it from Stout-authored rows. Same registry, same surface, no second-class treatment. The client owning their own analytical contributions to the engagement is what makes this a shared workspace and not a one-way feed.

The "Linked" chips repeat across surfaces. The same primitive lives on the trial-detail Stout's read, on catalysts, and here on materials. Clicking any chip lands on that entity, where its own Stout's read and Materials section apply. The cross-linking is the navigation structure that makes the registry feel like a graph rather than a folder hierarchy.
