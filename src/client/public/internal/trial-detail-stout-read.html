Pfizer/ Zelvox/ CARDIO-SHIELD

CARDIO-SHIELD

NCT05123456  ·  Heart failure with reduced EF
Phase 3 pivotal Recruiting LoE 2032
Primary intelligence

The pivotal that defines Pfizer's cardiometabolic franchise through 2030.

Updated 2026-04-21 by Stout

CARDIO-SHIELD is Pfizer's most consequential cardiometabolic readout this decade. The trial is over-enrolled, event accrual is running ahead of the protocol, and topline lands in late Q2 2026 (pulled in from prior Q3 read). Pfizer has been guiding to "first half" but the cadence we're tracking points to June, not May.

The base case is a positive primary endpoint with an effect size in line with the Phase 2b confidence intervals. The interesting question is the secondary: an SAP amendment in late 2024 loosened the composite definition, and Pfizer has not published the rationale. That's the line in the readout where the bull and bear cases diverge.

What to watch
  • Event accrual rate vs. protocol (signal: was the SAP amendment defensive).
  • Pfizer pre-announcing the readout window at JPM 2026.
  • Sell-side notes from CHF specialists post Apr 30 ECHO-HF interim.
  • FDA pre-NDA meeting minutes (should land late Q2).
Comparators
  • AZNVerzubrix / ECHO-HF
    Direct competitor; interim Apr 30. If continues, Pfizer's launch window narrows.
  • NVSCardexa
    Approved in HF-pEF, not HF-rEF. Different patient pool but shared payer conversation.
  • LLYTrizenta / GLOW-2
    Phase 3 readout 2027; Pfizer's window of exclusivity in HF-rEF runs ~14 months.
Implications
  • Asia-Pacific rights are unbundled in the current Pfizer co-promote with Sumitomo. A positive readout repositions that contract at renewal in 2027.
  • If positive, resets the cardiometabolic narrative against AstraZeneca and pulls PFE's R&D-to-launch productivity story back into the bull case for 2027 IR positioning.
  • Three small-cap follow-ons (Tenaya, Cytokinetics, Cardurion) become more dilutive vs. acquirable depending on the secondary endpoint magnitude.

Design notes. The page leads with the analyst voice. Pills, breadcrumb, and trial title give just enough orientation to know what trial you're on; everything below the fold is either Stout's interpretation or the structured facts that interpretation rests on. The markers table stays, but moves below the read because it's the supporting layer, not the headline.

Recent analyst activity is the visible heartbeat that proves the dashboard is being maintained. Each entry is a line item with the analyst's initials. This is what a client sees when they log in mid-quarter, and it is the strongest signal that "Stout publishes here, in real time" is true and not just a slide.

Implications (BD / Strategy / Licensing) is the structured field set you accepted. Three lanes, brand-700 lane labels, prose body. This is where the read crosses from "here's what we think happens" to "here's what to do about it," which is the deliverable a client is paying for.
