Clint Briefing
01 / What Clint is

The working surface and deliverable layer for pharma CI.

Clint sits on top of the data Stout already consumes and produces. It's where the CI work lives, and where clients see it in real time, all the time.

Live dashboard

Always current

Trial data refreshes from ClinicalTrials.gov directly. New analyst-curated events show up in the dashboard the moment they're saved, and the client sees them at the same time. The competitive picture reflects the latest event, not the last quarterly briefing.

Stout's analysis

Primary intelligence, published live

Stout's analysts publish primary analysis and intelligence directly into the platform. As notes are written, the client sees them in real time. With assembly already handled by Clint, analysts spend their time on interpretation and strategic reads, not formatting.

Whitelabel

A Stout-branded site per client

Each client gets a Stout-branded site, with engagement spaces themed inside it. Agency, client, and engagement-level branding are all supported.

Data model

Built around pharma

Companies, products, trials, catalysts, and events are core entities, with a source on every record. Dashboard, briefings, exports, and AI all read from the same data. Nothing gets retyped, nothing falls out of sync.

02 / What changes for Stout

What changes for Stout.

  • Faster kickoff. The competitive baseline for the target therapeutic area is already in the platform when the engagement starts. Analysts begin from a working picture and add Stout's curation and framing on top, instead of assembling from scratch.
  • Continuous presence. New events show up in the Stout-branded site as they happen, with Stout's primary analysis published alongside them. The client sees a live, annotated view all quarter, not just on delivery day.
  • The briefing focuses on strategy. Data review happens in the platform throughout the quarter, so the meeting opens at recommendations and decisions instead of slide narration.
  • Stickier renewals. A site that holds the client's annotations, watchlists, and past analysis has real switching cost. That's the difference between renewing the relationship and re-pitching it every year.
  • No engineering investment. Clint's team runs the ingest pipelines, source updates, infrastructure, and security. Stout uses the platform; Clint runs it.

04 / AI integration roadmap

Three ways to put AI on top of Clint's data.

Clint's data is already structured and cited, which is what AI needs to give reliable answers. Three ways to use it are on the roadmap.

For Stout's data team

Pull Clint data into Stout's own systems

Stout's data team can connect Clint to whatever they already use. Internal tools, AI workflows, data warehouses. The data flows out programmatically, with sources attached.

For analysts using AI assistants

Use Clint from inside Claude or Copilot

Ask Claude or Copilot a question about competitive data, and it answers from Clint with citations. Analysts who already use AI assistants get Clint's data inside their existing workflow, no separate tool to learn.

Inside the platform

AI built into Clint itself

Generate briefs, run scenario analysis, ask questions, all inside Clint. No need to send client data to outside AI tools.
