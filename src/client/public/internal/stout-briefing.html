02 / How it's built

Structured for pharma intelligence.

  • Pharma data, not free text. Companies, drugs, trials, catalysts, and events — categorized, connected, source-cited.
  • Events tied to drugs, trials, and companies. Readouts, regulatory actions, approvals, label changes, deal activity — each lands on the right place with a date and a source URL.
  • Built for fast reading. Phase bars, color-coded event markers, and a company-then-product layout. The competitive picture lands in seconds.
  • Same source for every deliverable. The interface, exports, briefing decks, client notices, and AI workflows all read from the same structured records. No copy-paste, no drift.

05 / Why this doesn't exist yet

A platform like this doesn't exist in advisory.

The components exist. The combination doesn't. Building it from scratch is a multi-year engineering lift — outside the natural shape of any obvious player.

What's missing in the market
  • Data vendors don't whitelabel. Evaluate, Citeline, and GlobalData hold structured pharma data, but they're built for end-users, not for advisory firms to rebrand and resell to their clients.
  • Advisory firms still deliver decks. CI engagements end in slides and documents, not in branded software clients log into through the engagement.
  • The combination doesn't exist anywhere. Structured pharma data + whitelabel delivery + AI-ready integration. No vendor and no advisory firm has assembled all three today.
What it takes to build from scratch
  • A real engineering team. Multiple senior full-stack engineers, a data engineer, and a pharma domain expert on staff — integrated, not contracted.
  • 12–18 months to a working product. On top of the time to hire the team. Multi-year commitment before any client value.
  • Ongoing data, engineering, and maintenance. Data licensing, ongoing hiring, and a maintained codebase — capabilities that aren't core to an advisory firm.
  • A multi-million-dollar build, then ongoing operating cost. Order of magnitude, not a specific quote — but it's a serious lift to start from zero.

06 / Where Stout's value sits in the platform

Stout's IP doesn't disappear. It moves into the platform.

The deliverable is a live, Stout-branded environment. The natural question becomes "where does Stout's analysis live?" Answer: it lives on top of the data, attached to it, not separate from it.

  • Curation. Watchlist, competitor set, asset prioritization, therapeutic-area framing — Stout's domain expertise applied to the data layer.
  • Annotation. Each meaningful event carries a Stout note: why it matters, what it shifts, what it implies for the client's positioning.
  • Strategy. Scenario analysis, deal implications, recommendations — sitting alongside the events that triggered them, all branded as Stout's work.
  • Switching cost. Watchlists, annotations, history, and scenario models live inside the Stout-branded environment. Leaving means losing institutional memory — which doesn't happen with static decks.
