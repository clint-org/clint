Engagement landing / what changed

Swap the company wordmark for a logo, shrink the NCT

The widget lives in the engagement landing's three-up grid alongside Next 14 days and Drafts. The column is narrow: roughly 460px at a 1440px viewport, 580px at 1920px. At that width the current row's metadata wraps onto a second line because the wordmark ("ELI LILLY"), product, NCT, and full timestamp all share the same line as the source chip.

Below: V0 is what ships today. V1-V3 replace the wordmark with a brand-tinted monogram tile and demote the NCT, in increasing levels of compression. Each option renders at both column widths so you can see how the meta line behaves under real constraints.

Constraint

Widget container is flex h-full flex-col border border-slate-200 bg-white inside grid grid-cols-3 gap-3.5. Inner padding is px-4. Row content has roughly 428px (1440 viewport) or 548px (1920 viewport) of horizontal space to work with after the icon column and the right-aligned source chip.

Orientations on V3

What else can we hide, flip, or replace

V3 already drops the NCT and the seconds off the timestamp. These four push further in different directions: collapse the two lines into one, flip identity below the summary, replace the drug name with the trial program name, or fold the event-type icon into the logo entirely. All shown at the narrow 460px column where decisions matter most.

O1

Single-line ticker · one row per event, no wrap

Collapse the two-line row into one. Logo, product, summary, and reltime sit on one line; the summary truncates with an ellipsis when it overflows. Full text appears on hover. Hides: the two-line structure, the source chip (binary noise), and the event-type icon (color on the delta carries it).

O2

Summary-led · identity moves below as a quiet sub-line

Flip the hierarchy. Summary reads first in slate-900; the meta line underneath shrinks to a 16px logo + product + reltime + a tiny source chip. The eye lands on the change first, identity is a footnote. The event-type icon stays but becomes secondary visually.

O3

When one sponsor dominates the feed (Eli Lilly is 4 of 5 events in your screenshot), repeating the logo on every row is redundant. Group consecutive events by company under a single tiny header strip (logo + company name + count). Rows beneath the header drop their own logo and indent slightly so the visual hierarchy reads. Singletons (Roche alone) still get a one-row group.

O4

Logo IS the row-icon · event-type lives in the delta color

Drop the event-type glyph (calendar, swap, plus) and let the logo occupy that column. The event-type is encoded by the color of the new value in the summary: green for date changes, violet for phase, red for status. Saves a column. Identity and event-type stop competing for the user's attention because they share one slot in the row.
