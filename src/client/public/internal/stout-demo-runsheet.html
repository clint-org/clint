11Pricing - frame it, don't dwell Deck · pricing~1:30 · cum 27:30
open deck pricing slide ↗

Say

Let me briefly touch on pricing. I built this because I saw a real gap. You had tools like Evaluate and Citeline to dive into datasets and run comparisons, but nothing that gave an agency like Stout a whitelabeled, live data-and-intelligence platform to deliver to clients - a true turnkey layer. That's the pain Clint solves.

But it's not priced like Evaluate or Citeline. Those, like most SaaS, charge per seat. With Clint, I think all your users should have access - every analyst and every client end user. So pricing is at the engagement level: as you win projects and onboard clients, you create spaces, and you pay for the number of spaces you deliver. As you bring more engagements onto Clint, the per-space price drops at certain bands.

I don't want to dwell on price. Once you're in the platform and it fits how you work, we can get into pricing and Stout's procurement process.

  • Deck pricing slide. Give the frame, then stop and open it up for questions.

If they ask - the objection bank

Ranked by how damaging they are unanswered. The top three you should preempt (see the ⚑ lines in beats 1, 4, 7). Answers in your voice - paraphrase.

  1. "If Pfizer watches CT.gov auto-populate this live, what stops them deciding the assembly was never worth paying us for?" beat 4/7 · HIGH
    Assembling the picture was never what they paid for - the read is. A live view makes you more present, every week vs every quarter, your name on every screen. Switching cost goes up, not down.
  2. "A wrong or stale date sits on a Stout-branded screen in front of my client. Who owns that?" beat 1/2 · HIGH
    Every event is confirmed vs projected (filled vs hollow) with a source on each; nothing the AI proposes publishes until an analyst reviews it; CT.gov re-syncs daily with a visible timestamp. You control what the client sees. (Lead with this - don't wait to be asked.)
  3. "Saves analyst time = we bill fewer hours." beat 3/7 · HIGH
    Redeployment, not reduction. Same hours, moved off assembly onto judgment, primary, and client time - and more engagements with the same team. The fee follows the value, and the value moves up.
  4. "If we stop paying - or you go under - what happens to a year of our briefs and history?" beat 10/11 · HIGH
    Your data is yours. Export to Excel/your tools today; on exit, a full export of briefs, events, and version history. Encrypted daily backups across two clouds. We'll put the exit terms in writing for the pilot. (This gates pilot → paid - have it crisp.)
  5. "Prove the tenant firewall. Pen-tested? Where does the data live? Who at Clint can see it?" beat 4/9 · HIGH
    Strict per-tenant and per-space firewalls - the same isolation that lets these demo spaces sit safely next to real ones. Explicit access only, AWS + Cloudflare, encrypted backups with rehearsed restores. Be honest on formal attestations (SOC 2 / pen-test) - share status and roadmap, don't overclaim.
  6. "What if the live import fails right now, on this call?" beat 6 · MED-HIGH
    "If the pull is slow I'll narrate it and cut to the pitch space - same result, a day later." The point is a graceful fallback, not the answer. (For a first run, you can skip the live import entirely.)
  7. "Why isn't this just a prettier Evaluate / Citeline?" beat 2/4 · MED
    They're data subscriptions you query. Clint is a whitelabeled delivery-and-annotation layer you control and brand, holding your primary intelligence and the client's engagement history. Neither incumbent does live client delivery under your brand.
  8. "Why shouldn't Stout just build this?" beat 8 · MED
    Focus, not cost. Building it is a couple of years and a pharma-tooling product team - not Stout's edge. Or start next week and keep your people on client work. (Don't get defensive - you'll have run the math too.)
  9. "Does the client ever see per-space pricing as a line item?" beat 11 · MED
    Never. It bundles into your engagement fee - not client-visible.
  10. "What's your involvement, and Sam's? Why is this intro happening?" any · MED
    Don't volunteer it. If asked: small advisory stake; the intro's happening because Sam thinks it's the right tool. Then move on - anything evasive costs trust.
  11. "Coverage gap vs the incumbents I already pay for?" beat 7 · MED
    Clint isn't a data vendor - it ingests CT.gov plus your own primary and secondary intelligence. You may still use Evaluate/Citeline for deep dataset dives; Clint is the delivery and annotation layer on top. (Be honest about the gap.)
  12. "MCP and the warehouse - live, or roadmap?" beat 8 · MED
    Phase 1 - everything I just showed - is live. MCP export, ask-in-space, and the warehouse are Phase 2, plainly roadmap. The one-paste import is live. (Blur this and he discounts the whole demo.)

MD verdict from the test run: would green-light a narrow free pilot on the strength of the rebid-stickiness + whitelabel-differentiation story. What moves it from pilot to paid: a credible answer on data accuracy/provenance under their brand and a clean data-exit guarantee, plus the redeployment framing. Nail those and the biggest risk (their reputation on your feed) becomes the reason they can't leave.
