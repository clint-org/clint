Sketch -- Marker Treatments

Telegraphing Stout's curation on the timeline.

Today an analyst-curated marker (Stout's primary intelligence) renders identically to a CT.gov-derived data marker. Same shape, same color, same weight. The intelligence layer is invisible at the level the eye actually parses.

Each treatment below adds a small additive signal so a Stout-curated marker reads as analyst commentary at a glance, while preserving the category color (green data, red regulatory, blue approval, amber commercial). All variants use the same six-marker reference row so they're directly comparable.

B

Halo ring

Thin slate ring offset 2px around analyst markers. Reads as a halo or focus state. Category color stays primary, halo is the secondary signal.

TrialCARDIO-SHIELDNCT05123456
How
Brand-700 ring, 1px stroke, 2-3px offset, 85% opacity. Works on any shape.
Tradeoff
Quiet but legible. Doesn't crowd the row. Reads ambiguously vs. a focus state, so focus styling needs a different cue.
C

Background plate

Soft brand-tinted rounded plate behind analyst markers. Treats the marker as sitting on a Stout-branded card.

TrialCARDIO-SHIELDNCT05123456
How
Brand-100 fill plate, brand-300 1px stroke, 4px corner radius, fits within 36px row height.
Tradeoff
Strong, brand-coded. Visually distinctive but introduces a competing color block; a row dense with analyst markers can feel busy.
D

Stout sigil

A small brand-colored dot tucked at the top-right of the marker. Tiny, additive, doesn't interact with the shape.

TrialCARDIO-SHIELDNCT05123456
How
3px brand-700 dot, white 1px stroke for separation, anchored top-right of the marker bounding box.
Tradeoff
Surgical, zero footprint shift. At 16px markers the dot reads as a notification badge; needs a tooltip-time gloss ("Stout-curated") to teach the meaning.
E

Brand underbar

A short brand-700 tick directly beneath analyst markers, between the marker and its date label. Echoes the accent bar in the deck.

How
2px brand-700 line, 14px wide, centered between marker and date label.
Tradeoff
Echoes the deck's accent bar idiom (brand line under section title). Pushes date 2px down, slightly reduces row capacity for stacked text.
F

Halo + sigil (combined)

B and D together. Halo carries the soft "this is curated" cue; the sigil makes the curator unambiguous on close inspection.

TrialCARDIO-SHIELDNCT05123456
How
B and D together. Halo for ambient parsing, sigil for explicit attribution.
Tradeoff
Loudest of the set. Most legible at a distance. May overcorrect if the row is heavily Stout-curated; consider gating the sigil to selected markers (e.g., feature flag in tooltip but on by default at the row level).
Recommendation. Variant B (halo) is the lightest move that does the job. Add the sigil from D only on the Stout-branded whitelabel views, where attribution is the entire pitch. Variant C (plate) is too loud for default; reserve for a "highlighted analyst event" treatment in the briefing or executive read.

Open question. What signal flips a marker into the Stout-curated bucket? Today the only proxy is "marker not created by the CT.gov sync job," but that mixes in client-entered markers too. Cleanest answer is a new field on the marker record (e.g., curated_by_role: 'sync' | 'analyst' | 'client') populated at creation, not inferred.
